# Nighttime Safety Environment Checklist

Reference for the Sleep Pattern Expert skill.
Use when bed exits are frequent or when fall risk during nighttime
movement is a concern. All items are environmental — no medical advice.

---

## Bedroom

- [ ] Nightlight installed — motion-activated preferred; red/amber light
      (not blue/white) to minimize circadian disruption
- [ ] Clear path from bed to bedroom door — no rugs, cables, or furniture
      corners that could catch a foot
- [ ] Non-slip mat beside the bed where the elder first steps down
- [ ] Non-slip slippers within arm's reach of the bed
- [ ] Bed height appropriate — elder's feet should rest flat on the floor
      when sitting on the edge; too high or too low both increase fall risk
- [ ] Bed rail or grab bar if the elder has difficulty rising from bed

## Hallway / Path to Bathroom

- [ ] Continuous lighting coverage from bedroom to bathroom —
      no dark gaps in the path
- [ ] Floor is clear of obstacles (shoes, bags, pet items)
- [ ] Hallway width allows the elder to move without bumping walls

## Bathroom

- [ ] Motion-activated nightlight or light switch accessible immediately
      upon entry
- [ ] Non-slip mat on the floor in front of the toilet
- [ ] Grab bar beside the toilet (at a height the elder can grip while
      seated and while rising)
- [ ] Non-slip surface in shower or bathtub
- [ ] Grab bar in shower/tub area
- [ ] Toilet seat height appropriate (raised seat available if needed)

## Quick-Action Items (High Priority)

If nighttime bathroom trips are frequent, prioritize these three:

1. **Motion-sensor nightlight from bed to bathroom** — single highest-impact change
2. **Non-slip mat at bedside** — first step out of bed is the highest risk moment
3. **Grab bar at toilet** — rising from seated position at night is a common fall point

---

## Framing for Families

When suggesting improvements, use warm observational language:

- "One idea some families find helpful is..."
- "A small change that can make nighttime movement a bit safer is..."
- "You might consider checking whether the path from the bedroom to the
  bathroom has a gentle nightlight — it can make a big difference."

Never say: "This is a fall risk" / "The elder needs safety modifications"
(clinical and deficit-framing language).
