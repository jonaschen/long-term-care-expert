# Home Modification Reference

Accessible home modification checklist for the Mobility and Fall Risk Expert skill.
Prioritized by fall prevention impact. All items are environmental — no medical advice.

---

## Quick Wins (Low Cost, High Impact)

These can be done within a day and have immediate safety benefit:

| Modification | Location | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Motion-sensor nightlights | Bedroom → hallway → bathroom path | Most nighttime falls happen on the path to the bathroom |
| Non-slip mat at bedside | Bedroom floor beside bed | First step out of bed is the highest-risk moment |
| Remove loose rugs | All rooms | Tripping edges are a leading cause of falls |
| Secure rug edges with double-sided tape | Any kept rugs | Eliminates the curling edge trip hazard |
| Clear walking paths | All rooms | 30-minute declutter session eliminates most obstacle hazards |
| Non-slip bath mat | Bathroom floor | Simple replacement, large impact |

---

## Medium-Term Modifications (Require Planning or Installation)

| Modification | Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Grab bar beside toilet | Bathroom | Must be wall-mounted into studs, not suction cups; position at a height comfortable for the elder's arm when seated |
| Grab bar in shower/tub | Bathroom | Angled grab bar allows grip during both entry/exit and while standing |
| Non-slip strips in shower | Shower/tub floor | Self-adhesive strips; replace annually as adhesive weakens |
| Raised toilet seat | Bathroom | Useful when elder struggles to lower/rise; available at pharmacy supply shops |
| Handrail addition on stairs | Stairs | If only one handrail, add on the dominant hand side for rising |
| Non-slip stair nosing strips | Stairs | Apply to the edge of each step |
| Bed height adjustment | Bedroom | Use bed risers or remove bed frame to achieve correct height |
| Rearrange furniture | Living areas | Create clear, wide paths; ensure the elder can touch a wall or furniture surface throughout their main movement route |

---

## Lighting Assessment

Poor lighting is a major, often overlooked fall risk. Check each area:

- **Bedroom:** Is it possible to see clearly enough to navigate at night
  without turning on bright overhead lights?
- **Hallway:** Are there dark stretches between rooms?
- **Bathroom:** Can the elder see clearly when entering at night?
- **Stairs:** Is the top and bottom of every staircase well-lit?
- **Entrance:** Is the entry/exit well-lit after dark?

**Solution approach:** Motion-activated LED lights (warm color, not
blue-white) placed at ankle height are the most effective nighttime
solution — they light the path without fully waking the elder.

## Furniture Assessment

| Problem | Observation | Simple Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Sofa too low/soft | Elder struggles to rise from couch | Add a firm cushion; consider a chair with armrests as primary seating |
| Chair no armrests | Elder uses table edge or wall to push up | Add a chair with armrests for daily use |
| Coffee table in walking path | Elder navigates around it | Reposition or temporarily remove |
| Bed too high | Elder's feet dangle when seated on edge | Remove bed frame or add step beside bed |
| Bed too low | Elder struggles to rise | Add bed risers |

---

## Suggested Sequence for Families

If the elder's movement patterns are changing, consider this order:

1. **Day 1–2:** Lighting and rug safety (nightlights, remove/secure rugs)
2. **Week 1:** Bathroom safety (non-slip mat, check for grab bar needs)
3. **Month 1:** Assess and install grab bars if needed; adjust bed/chair height
4. **Ongoing:** Monthly walk-through to identify new hazards as elder's
   patterns change

---

## Framing for Families

"Making the home a bit more comfortable to move around in is one of the
simplest things families can do — and it doesn't need to happen all at once."

"Some families start with just one or two changes and find they make a
noticeable difference in how comfortable the elder feels moving around."
