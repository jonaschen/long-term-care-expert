## Footwear Assessment and Selection

Choosing the right footwear is a simple but highly effective way to improve an elder's stability both inside and outside the home. You might consider shoes that have a snug fit and provide good support around the heel and arch. Soles should be thin enough to allow the elder to feel the ground but must be made of a non-slip material to provide adequate traction on smooth surfaces. Velcro straps or elastic laces are often better than traditional laces, as they provide a secure fit without the risk of untied strings becoming a trip hazard.

It is helpful to avoid loose-fitting slippers, backless shoes, or walking in socks on smooth floors, as these significantly increase the risk of a slip. If the elder prefers wearing indoor shoes, ensure they have a firm sole and a closed heel. Regularly check the condition of the elder's shoes for signs of wear; if the soles have become smooth or the support has weakened, it is time to consider a new pair.

Observe how the elder walks in different shoes. If you notice they seem to shuffle or appear more unsteady in a particular pair, it may be because those shoes are not providing enough support or grip. Compared to the usual pattern of choosing footwear based only on comfort, prioritizing these safety features can help the elder feel more secure on their feet. Encouraging the use of appropriate footwear is a small environmental adjustment that can lead to a much safer daily routine.
