## Living Room and Common Areas

The living room is where many daily activities occur, so maintaining clear pathways is essential. You might consider arranging furniture to provide wide, unobstructed walking paths that accommodate a cane or walker if the elder uses one. Ensure that all rugs are either removed or secured firmly to the floor using double-sided tape or non-slip backing to prevent the edges from curling. Electrical and telephone cords should be routed along walls and away from walking areas. If the elder seems to prefer sitting in a particular chair, ensure it is sturdy, has armrests to assist with standing, and is not too low. Compared to the usual pattern, if the elder appears to avoid certain areas of the room, check for subtle tripping hazards like uneven floor transitions or loose wires. Maintaining a well-organized space allows the elder to move with greater confidence throughout the day.
