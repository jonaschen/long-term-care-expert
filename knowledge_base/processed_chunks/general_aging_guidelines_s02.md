## How to Help an Elder Maintain Independence in Daily Activities

Maintaining independence is one of the most important aspects of wellbeing for an elder. This independence is often built through the small, repetitive tasks of daily life. When an elder performs their own self-care—such as dressing, grooming, or preparing a simple snack—they are engaging in essential movements that keep their joints flexible and their muscles active. Families can support this by encouraging the elder to handle these routines themselves for as long as they feel comfortable.

Instead of taking over a task to save time, consider how you can adapt the environment to make it easier for the elder to succeed. For example, placing commonly used kitchen items at waist height can help an elder continue to prepare their own tea or light meals without needing to reach or bend excessively. Observing how an elder handles these daily tasks can give you valuable insights into their comfort levels. If they seem to be struggling with a particular routine, you might consider a small adjustment, like adding a sturdy chair in the bathroom or using a long-handled tool for dressing.

Independence is also supported by 'active living' choices. Encourage the elder to walk to the mailbox or help with light household chores like folding laundry. These activities provide a sense of accomplishment and keep the body in motion. When an elder feels they can still contribute to the household and take care of their own needs, their confidence grows. This confidence, in turn, makes them more likely to stay active and engaged in their community, creating a positive cycle of independence and health.
