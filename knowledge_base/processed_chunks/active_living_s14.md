## Understanding Physical Effort Levels

Activities can be classified into different levels of effort, and it's helpful to understand which ones the elder is doing. 'Light' activities include basic self-care like brushing teeth or slow walking around the house. 'Moderate' activities, such as brisk walking, mopping, or light gardening, should make the elder breathe a bit faster and feel warmer but still able to talk comfortably.

'Vigorous' activities are more intense and might include fast walking, climbing stairs with heavy items, or aerobics. These will make the elder breathe quite hard. For most older adults, focusing on a mix of light and moderate activities is the best pattern for maintaining health. You might consider observing the elder's effort level during different tasks; if they seem to be in the 'extreme effort' zone for basic tasks, the activity may need to be simplified. Understanding these levels helps in planning a routine that is effective yet safe.
