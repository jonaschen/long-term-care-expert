## Integrating Housework into a Fitness Routine

Daily chores offer excellent opportunities for movement that doesn't feel like 'exercise'. Many household tasks can be modified to provide more physical benefits. For example, when mopping the floor or vacuuming, the elder can use larger, more deliberate motions to engage their arms and core. Carrying a bag of groceries using the forearm or shoulder rather than just the fingers can help build upper body endurance.

In the kitchen, the elder can practice balancing or mini-squats while waiting for water to boil. Using slightly heavier ceramic bowls or plates instead of light plastic ones during meals can also provide a small amount of resistance training for the hands and arms. If the elder seems to enjoy gardening, activities like watering plants or light weeding provide great opportunities for bending, reaching, and squatting. These lifestyle-based movements are easy to maintain because they are part of the elder's existing daily pattern.
