## Supporting Independence through Daily Activity

Daily chores are excellent opportunities to support the elder's ability to live independently. Activities like hanging laundry, carrying groceries, or tidying the living space are functional movements that naturally maintain balance and muscle tone. When the elder participates in these routine tasks, they are practicing the coordination needed for daily living. You might consider encouraging the elder to perform these tasks at their own pace. If the elder seems to find certain tasks more "strenuous" than before, it may be helpful to simplify the activity rather than removing it entirely. Staying involved in household rhythms helps the elder feel like a contributing member of the family, which supports their overall sense of purpose and functional capability.
