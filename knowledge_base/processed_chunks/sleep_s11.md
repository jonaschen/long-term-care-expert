## Managing Evening Stimulants and Diet

What an elder consumes in the hours leading up to bedtime can have a significant impact on their ability to settle down. While many people enjoy a cup of tea or a small snack in the evening, certain ingredients can act as hidden stimulants. Caffeine, found in coffee, many teas, and even some chocolates, can stay in the system for several hours and may prevent the elder from reaching deep sleep stages. It is often helpful to switch to caffeine-free options after lunch.

Heavy or spicy meals close to bedtime can also cause discomfort or indigestion, which may lead to frequent wake-ups. Instead, if the elder is hungry in the evening, a light, easy-to-digest snack is a better choice. It is also worth observing the elder's response to different foods and drinks. Some may find that certain evening habits, while seemingly harmless, consistently lead to a more restless night. Families can support the elder by offering soothing alternatives like warm milk or herbal infusions that do not contain stimulants. By making small, mindful adjustments to the evening diet, the elder’s body can more easily enter a state of relaxation, paving the way for a more stable and refreshing night’s sleep.
