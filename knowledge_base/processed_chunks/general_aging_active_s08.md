## When to Consult a Professional

While staying active is generally beneficial, families should be observant of signs that indicate the elder should stop and rest. If the elder experiences dizziness, chest discomfort, unusual shortness of breath, or cold sweats during or after movement, the activity should be paused immediately. Other signs to watch for include sudden blurred vision, ear ringing, or joint discomfort that lasts for more than an hour after the activity ends. If these patterns continue or appear frequently, it is a good idea for the family to accompany the elder for a conversation with a professional. Observing these changes early allows for the routine to be adjusted to better suit the elder's current physical state.
