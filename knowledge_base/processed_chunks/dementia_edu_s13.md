## Community Support Resources in Taiwan

Families in Taiwan have access to a variety of community resources designed to support elders with memory changes and their caregivers. You might consider reaching out to local 'Day Care Centers' or 'Memory Schools' (such as Wisdom Classes), which provide structured activities and social opportunities for elders in a safe environment. these programs can help slow the loss of daily living skills and provide valuable social engagement.

The '1966' Long-Term Care Hotline is a vital resource for information on government-subsidized services, including home care, professional nursing assistance, and transportation for medical visits. You can also contact the Taiwan Dementia Care Association (TDCA) for counseling and educational resources. Many local community centers offer 'family caregiver support stations' where you can learn practical care skills and connect with support groups. For safety, you might consider applying for an 'Anti-Lost Bracelet' or an NFC-enabled identification tag through your local social bureau or the Federation for the Welfare of the Elderly. These resources are designed to provide a safety net for families, helping you navigate the challenges of caregiving with the support of a broader community. Taking advantage of these services can reduce the feeling of being alone and provide practical help for everyday challenges.
