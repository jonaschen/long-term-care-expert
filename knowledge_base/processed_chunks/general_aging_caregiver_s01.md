## Caregiver Wellbeing and Recognizing Burnout

Supporting an elder whose behavioral patterns have changed is a journey that requires significant emotional and physical energy. It is common for family members to focus entirely on the elder, often neglecting their own needs until they reach a point of exhaustion. Recognizing the early signs of burnout is the first step in maintaining a healthy environment for everyone in the home. You might notice that you are feeling more irritable than usual, experiencing changes in your own sleep patterns, or pulling away from social activities you once enjoyed.

When the weight of caregiving begins to feel overwhelming, it is important to acknowledge that these feelings are a natural response to a demanding role. If you find yourself frequently feeling resentful or physically depleted, this is a signal that your current pace may not be sustainable. Caregiving is a long-term commitment, and your wellbeing is the foundation upon which the elder's support rests. Taking time to check in with yourself daily—even for just a few minutes—can help you stay aware of your emotional limits. Remember that a caregiver who is rested and supported is better equipped to provide the gentle, patient presence that the elder needs. If these feelings of exhaustion persist, it is a sign that you should consider reaching out for additional help or exploring local resources to share the responsibilities.
