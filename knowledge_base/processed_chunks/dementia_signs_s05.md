## Changes in Decision Making and Safety

You might notice the elder making choices that seem out of character or indicate a decrease in situational awareness. This often manifests as a lack of judgment regarding the environment, personal safety, or social norms. These behavioral changes are not intentional but reflect a shift in how the elder processes information and assesses risks.

**Daily Life Example:** On a sweltering summer day in Taiwan, the elder might put on a heavy wool sweater and a thick jacket because they cannot accurately gauge the temperature. They might also purchase large quantities of spoiled or unnecessary food items at the market, or become less cautious about sharing personal information with strangers.

**Gentle Supportive Action:** Instead of arguing about their choices, which can lead to frustration, try to modify the environment to make the safe choice the easiest one. For example, you can pre-arrange the elder's wardrobe so that only seasonally appropriate clothing is easily accessible in the front of the closet, making it simpler for them to choose correctly without a confrontation.
