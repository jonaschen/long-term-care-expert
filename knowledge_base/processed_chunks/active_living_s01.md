## Weekly Activity Goals for Older Adults

Maintaining a regular pattern of movement is essential for long-term health. The elder should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity movement per week. This can be broken down into smaller sessions, such as 10 to 30 minutes at a time, spread across the week. If the elder prefers more vigorous activities, the goal can be adjusted to 75 minutes per week.

A balanced routine includes several types of activities: muscle strengthening, balance improvement, and flexibility. Compared to a sedentary pattern, adding even small amounts of movement can significantly enhance daily energy levels. You might consider helping the elder track their progress using a simple weekly log. If the elder seems to find 150 minutes too challenging initially, they can start with shorter durations and gradually increase the time as their comfort grows. The key is consistency rather than intensity at the beginning.
