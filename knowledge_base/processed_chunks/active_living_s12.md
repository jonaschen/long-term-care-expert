## Community Mobility and Outdoor Safety

Remaining active in the community helps the elder stay connected and physically fit. Walking to a nearby market or taking the bus are great ways to integrate movement into the day. When outdoors, it is important to be mindful of the environment. The elder should look around carefully, especially in crowded areas or narrow pathways, to avoid sudden drops or collisions.

Using a bus or train provides opportunities to practice balance. For example, if it is safe, the elder can try standing for part of the journey while holding onto a pole or handle. When shopping, using a backpack instead of carrying bags in one hand can help distribute weight more evenly and keep both hands free for balance. If the elder seems hesitant to go out alone, you might consider arranging for a friend or family member to accompany them. Staying mobile in the community supports both physical health and a sense of belonging.
