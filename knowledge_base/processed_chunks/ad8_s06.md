## Handling Household Finances and Bills

This area tracks the elder’s ability to manage more complicated financial tasks that they previously handled with ease. This includes things like balancing a simple household budget, paying monthly utility bills on time, or managing a bank account independently.

Concrete daily-life examples:
- Finding a stack of unpaid bills or "past due" notices that were previously handled promptly and organized by the elder.
- Making noticeable errors in basic addition or subtraction when trying to calculate change at a market or local shop.
- Expressing significant confusion or anxiety when looking at a bank statement or a common notice that they used to understand clearly.
- Having difficulty explaining how much money they have available for their daily needs, despite having a steady and predictable income.

How to gently observe:
Offer to help "organize the mail" or "sort through the papers on the kitchen table" together as a helpful gesture. This allows you to see if bills are being missed or if they seem overwhelmed by financial documents that they used to manage without any help, all without directly questioning their competence or independence.
