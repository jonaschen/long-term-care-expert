## Benefits of Staying Active for Overall Wellbeing

Staying active is a key pillar of aging well. Regular physical movement helps maintain body strength and energy levels, which naturally change over time. By incorporating at least 150 minutes of moderate activity into the weekly routine, an elder can support their heart health and emotional state. The elder seems to have more vitality when they avoid sitting for long periods, such as watching television for more than two hours at a time. Movement is not just about exercise; it is about maintaining a body that feels capable and a mind that stays sharp. Compared to the usual pattern of inactivity, a more active lifestyle contributes to a sense of accomplishment and physical comfort. Families can encourage these habits by highlighting how good the elder feels after a short walk or a light gardening session.
