## Protecting Assets and Legal Safety

As memory patterns change, an elder may become more vulnerable to financial confusion or external scams. It is important to observe if the elder is struggling with everyday tasks like paying bills or managing their bank account. To protect their financial wellbeing, you might consider having a calm family discussion about future planning while the elder is still able to express their wishes. You might consider setting up joint accounts or establishing a 'Property Trust' to ensure that their assets are managed safely and used for their care.

In Taiwan, the legal system provides several ways to protect elders. For instance, you can apply for a 'Guardianship' or 'Assistance' declaration through the courts if the elder can no longer make safe decisions for themselves. This ensures that a trusted person is legally responsible for their financial and legal matters. It is also helpful to establish a good relationship with the elder's local bank and neighbors, who can alert you to any unusual activity. Educating the elder about common scams and helping them manage their phone and mail can also prevent unwanted financial loss. By taking these proactive steps early, you provide a secure foundation for the elder's future, ensuring that their resources are protected and their needs are met throughout their journey.
