## Walking for Health and Vitality

Walking is one of the most accessible forms of activity for older adults. For the best results, the elder should aim for sessions of 10 minutes or longer. To increase the challenge and support balance, you might consider different walking patterns. For example, the elder can try walking in a straight line along the floor tiles or taking slightly larger steps than usual.

The pace should be moderate—fast enough that the elder feels a bit warmer and breathes more deeply, but still able to hold a conversation. If the elder feels confident, they can try walking with a heel-to-toe pattern to further support their stability. When walking outdoors, ensure they are wearing appropriate footwear and are aware of their surroundings, such as uneven pavement or traffic. If the elder seems to tire quickly, encourage them to take frequent, short walks throughout the day rather than one long one.
