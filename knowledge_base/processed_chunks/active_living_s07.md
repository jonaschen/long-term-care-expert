## Core Stability and Balance Exercises

Strong core and balance patterns are vital for preventing falls and maintaining independence. These exercises can be done while standing or sitting. A simple balance exercise is standing with both feet together and trying to maintain a steady posture without swaying. As the elder improves, they can try shifting their weight slightly or even standing on one leg while holding onto a sturdy support like a table or chair.

Twisting movements also support core strength. For example, placing an item slightly behind and to the side, then having the elder slowly turn their torso to reach for it, is a practical way to integrate core work. The elder should hold the twist for 10 to 30 seconds on each side. If the elder seems to wobble, always ensure they have a firm grip on a support. Gradually reducing the amount of support used can help build greater confidence in their stability over time.
