## Knowing When to Seek Extra Support

Observing changes in the elder’s behavioral patterns is a key part of caregiving. You might consider keeping a simple log of how the elder moves and any times they seem to lose their balance. If you observe a persistent pattern where the elder has more difficulty walking than usual, or if their pace has significantly slowed down compared to their usual pattern, it may be helpful to review their daily routine. If the elder seems to have frequent near-misses or expresses a new fear of falling, these are important indicators. In such cases, you might consider accompanying the elder to a community health center for a general wellness check and to discuss their mobility. Lifestyle adjustments, such as reviewing their activity levels or home environment with a professional, can provide valuable guidance for maintaining long-term safety and independence.
