## Bathroom Safety Modifications

The bathroom is a high-priority area for fall prevention due to the frequent presence of water and slippery surfaces. To improve safety, you might consider placing high-friction non-slip mats or adhesive anti-slip strips both inside the shower and on the bathroom floor. It is helpful to ensure a dry mat is placed right outside the bathing area to catch moisture from the elder's feet immediately. Installing sturdy grab bars near the toilet and inside the shower provides a reliable point of support. Compared to the usual pattern, if the elder seems to hesitate or reaches for the sink or towel rack for balance, it is a sign that dedicated support bars are needed. Keep the floor as dry as possible at all times and ensure that lighting is bright enough to reveal any hidden wet spots. If you observe the elder taking longer to transition from sitting to standing in the bathroom, a raised toilet seat might also be a helpful environmental adjustment to consider.
