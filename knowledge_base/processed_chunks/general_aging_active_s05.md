## Adapting Activities to Changing Abilities

As physical capacity naturally shifts, activities should be adapted to remain safe and effective. The elder might transition from "advanced" movements to "basic" versions if they feel more "breathless" or "strained" than usual. For example, if walking a long distance feels too much, a shorter walk with frequent rests or a seated stretching routine can be substituted. It is important to monitor how much effort the elder is exerting. A simple way to gauge this is by observing if they can still talk while moving; if they are too breathless to speak, the activity might be too intense. By adjusting the duration or intensity, the elder can continue to enjoy the benefits of movement without overexertion.
