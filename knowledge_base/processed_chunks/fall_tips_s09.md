## Gentle Exercise for Balance and Strength

Regular physical activity is key to maintaining the strength and balance needed to prevent falls. You might consider encouraging the elder to engage in gentle movements that focus on the lower limbs, such as rising onto their tiptoes while holding a sturdy surface or performing slow, controlled half-squats while using a chair for support. Activities like Tai Chi are also excellent for improving coordination and stability. Aiming for consistent activity throughout the week, even in short 10-minute sessions, can make a difference. Compared to the usual pattern, if the elder seems to tire more easily or has difficulty standing on one leg even with support, it may be helpful to focus more on these gentle exercises. If this behavioral pattern continues, you might consider consulting a professional for suggestions on lifestyle activities that support overall wellbeing and physical confidence.
