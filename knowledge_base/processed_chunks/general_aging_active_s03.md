## Encouraging Engagement and Motivation

Motivation often stems from a "heart-centered" approach to life. To help an elder stay engaged, it is useful to mix different types of activities that provide joy. This "puzzle" of a healthy lifestyle includes brain-stimulating activities like reading or board games, social interactions such as visiting friends, and physical movement like stretching or brisk walking. If an elder seems reluctant to start, try focusing on the social or mental aspects first. A sense of determination and persistence can be built by setting small, achievable goals, like walking for ten minutes twice a day. When movement feels like a natural part of a fulfilling day rather than a chore, the elder is more likely to maintain the routine long-term.
