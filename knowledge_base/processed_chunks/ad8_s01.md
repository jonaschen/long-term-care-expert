## Noticing Changes in Judgment and Decision Making

This domain focuses on whether the elder is having more trouble making sound choices compared to their usual patterns. It’s not about occasional mistakes, which everyone makes, but rather a noticeable shift in their ability to evaluate situations, weigh consequences, and make decisions that align with their own long-term interests and safety.

Concrete daily-life examples:
- Buying items they clearly don't need or already have in abundance, such as purchasing three extra vacuum cleaners when they already have a working one.
- Giving away unusually large amounts of money to strangers, telemarketers, or unverified organizations that they would have ignored in the past.
- Choosing clothing that is completely inappropriate for the current weather, such as wearing a heavy wool coat on a humid mid-summer afternoon.
- Difficulty identifying a potential scam or an obviously suspicious phone call that they would have easily spotted and dismissed before.

How to gently observe:
You might notice this while shopping together or discussing a recent purchase. If they mention a "great deal" that sounds suspicious, observe how they explain their reasoning. Instead of correcting them immediately, ask gentle questions like, "That's interesting, what made you decide on that one?" This helps you understand if their logic has shifted without creating a confrontation.
