## Leg Strengthening for Daily Mobility

Strong legs are the foundation for walking, climbing stairs, and rising from a chair. One effective exercise is the 'half-squat'. While holding onto a sturdy surface, the elder can slowly lower their hips as if they were about to sit, then stand back up. Repeating this several times builds power in the thighs and glutes.

Another simple activity is the 'slow sit-to-stand'. When the elder is about to sit down, they should do so as slowly as possible, taking about 15 to 30 seconds to complete the motion. This keeps the leg muscles engaged for a longer period. For those who want a greater challenge, you might consider using 500g sandbags around the ankles during these movements. If the elder seems to have trouble with their knees, ensure they do not squat too deeply and always keep their feet flat on the floor. These activities support overall bone health and mobility.
