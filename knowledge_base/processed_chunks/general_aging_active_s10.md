## Integrating Functional Strength into Daily Life

Strengthening the body does not always require a gym; it can be integrated into everyday moments. Reaching for a high shelf can be a way to stretch the shoulders, while slowly sitting down and standing up from a chair can build leg strength. Even waiting for a bus is an opportunity to practice balance by standing tall or gently shifting weight from one foot to the other. You might consider suggesting these "micro-movements" to the elder as a way to stay strong without feeling like they are "exercising." By turning daily life into a series of small, beneficial movements, the elder maintains their functional capacity in a way that feels natural and manageable.
