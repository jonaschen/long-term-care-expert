## Assistive Device Selection and Maintenance

When an elder's movement patterns change, assistive devices like canes or walkers can provide essential support and confidence. It is important to ensure that the device is the correct height for the elder. When standing upright with arms at their sides, the handle of the cane or walker should generally be at the level of the elder's wrist. This allows for a slight bend in the elbow and provides the most effective support without causing strain to the shoulders or back.

Regular maintenance of these devices is a key part of environmental safety. You might consider checking the rubber tips (ferrules) at the bottom of canes and walkers once a month. These tips can become worn or smooth over time, reducing their grip on slippery surfaces. If the rubber appears cracked or has uneven wear, it should be replaced immediately. It is also helpful to ensure that the handles are clean and provide a secure, non-slip grip.

Observe how the elder uses the device. If they seem to be leaning too heavily on it or if the device is often left behind in another room, it may not be the right fit for their current needs. You might consider a behavioral observation: does the elder use the device consistently, or only when they feel especially tired? Encouraging regular use of a well-maintained device helps the elder maintain a consistent level of stability and reduces the risk of an unexpected stumble in the home.
