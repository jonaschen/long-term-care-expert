## Safe Exercise Preparation and Warm-Up

Preparation is key to a safe movement session. The elder should wear comfortable, well-fitting clothing and flat-soled shoes with good grip. Avoid wearing only socks on smooth floors. Before starting, it is helpful to drink a small amount of water and ensure the room is well-ventilated.

Every session should begin with a gentle warm-up to prepare the body. This might include light reaching, slow walking in place, or gentle neck and shoulder rotations. During these activities, the elder should be encouraged to breathe naturally and avoid holding their breath. Counting out loud during movements is a good way to ensure a steady breathing rhythm. If the elder seems to be holding their breath, remind them to count to four with each motion. Starting slowly allows the body to adjust and helps prevent discomfort during the more active parts of the routine.
