## Awareness of the Current Month and Year

This domain observes if the elder can consistently keep track of the current month and year. Occasional slips happen to everyone, especially during busy times or vacations, but a persistent pattern of being several months or even years off is a notable change in their orientation to time.

Concrete daily-life examples:
- Referring to the current year as if it were five or ten years ago, perhaps mentioning people who are no longer around as if they were coming over today.
- Believing it is the middle of winter when it is actually a very hot and humid summer afternoon, and perhaps dressing in heavy layers accordingly.
- Asking when a major holiday like the Lunar New Year is occurring when that holiday actually passed several weeks or months prior.
- Writing the wrong year on a document or a simple note repeatedly, even after seeing the correct date on a calendar or phone.

How to gently observe:
During casual, everyday conversation, you can mention an upcoming event by saying something like, "I can't believe it's already March; the year is going by so fast." Observe if they seem surprised by the month mentioned or if they confidently mention a completely different time of year. You can also glance at a wall calendar together to see if they can identify the current date naturally.
