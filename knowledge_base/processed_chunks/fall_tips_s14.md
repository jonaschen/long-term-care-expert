## Maintenance of Mobility Aids

If the elder uses a cane or a walker, regular maintenance is required to ensure the equipment provides the intended support. You might consider checking the rubber tips at the bottom of these aids for wear and tear; if the tread is worn down, the aid may slip on smooth floors. Ensure that the height of the cane or walker is adjusted correctly so that the elder’s elbow is slightly bent when holding it. Keep the handles clean and free of grease to ensure a firm grip. If you observe the elder leaning heavily to one side or if the walker seems to wobble, it may indicate that the aid is not properly adjusted or is in need of repair. Regularly reviewing the condition of these tools helps ensure they remain a reliable behavioral support for the elder’s daily mobility and physical confidence.
