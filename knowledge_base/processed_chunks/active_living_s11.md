## Safe Stair Climbing for Leg Strength

Climbing stairs is a powerful way to support leg health and heart function. When possible, you might consider encouraging the elder to use the stairs instead of the elevator for one or two floors. Safety is paramount, so the elder should always use the handrail for support, especially if they haven't climbed stairs in a long time.

To increase the benefit, the elder can practice taking one step at a time and standing tall on each step before moving to the next. For a more advanced challenge, they can try taking two steps at once if they have a companion for safety. If the elder seems to get winded easily, they should stop and rest on the landing. Gradually reducing the reliance on the handrail (e.g., using only a few fingers instead of the whole hand) can help improve balance. Regular stair use is a great way to maintain functional independence and lower body power.
