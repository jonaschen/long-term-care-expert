## Framing Observations with Warmth and Respect

When you notice a change in an elder's behavior, how you think about that change and how you eventually discuss it matters greatly. Keeping the tone warm, observational, and respectful helps maintain the elder's dignity and reduces their potential for anxiety or defensiveness.

Strategies for warm framing:
- Use "we" and "us": Frame observations as something the entire family is noticing together as a team to support the elder. For example, "We've noticed the remote control has been a bit tricky lately; let's see if we can find a simpler one together."
- Focus on support, not correction: Instead of saying "You forgot your appointment again," try a more supportive approach: "It looks like the schedule got a bit mixed up today. Let's work on a way to make the wall calendar even easier for all of us to read."
- Validate their feelings: If the elder expresses frustration about a mistake or a moment of confusion, acknowledge it gently. "It really can be frustrating when things don't go as planned. Let's take a little break and try again later when we're both feeling refreshed."
- Keep the environment calm: Important observations and discussions should happen in a relaxed, private atmosphere. Avoid bringing up concerns during times of high stress or when others are present, as this can lead to embarrassment.
