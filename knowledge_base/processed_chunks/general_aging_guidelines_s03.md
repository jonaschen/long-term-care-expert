## Nutrition and Meal Habits That Support Healthy Aging

A balanced approach to eating is the fuel that supports an elder’s daily activity. Families should focus on creating a meal environment that is both nutritious and enjoyable, emphasizing whole foods and consistent patterns. A helpful guideline recommended by health professionals is the "Healthy Plate" concept, which suggests filling the plate with a variety of colors and textures. You might consider the rule of "more vegetables than fruit" and aiming for a portion of protein that is about the size of the elder's palm at each meal.

Protein is particularly important for supporting the elder's muscle strength. You might encourage the elder to include soy milk, eggs, or soft-cooked meats in their daily diet. If you notice the elder seems to be eating less than usual, consider the texture of the food. Sometimes, simply cutting vegetables into smaller pieces or cooking grains until they are softer can make a meal much more comfortable and appealing. Adjusting the texture ensures the elder can still enjoy a wide variety of foods regardless of changes in dental comfort.

Beyond what is on the plate, the social aspect of eating plays a major role in healthy aging. Eating together can turn a meal into a highlight of the day, encouraging the elder to stay at the table longer and maintain a more consistent intake. Observe the elder's mealtime habits—if they seem to be losing interest in food when eating alone, a shared family meal can often provide the gentle encouragement they need. By focusing on balanced nutrition and a supportive mealtime environment, you help the elder maintain the energy needed for an active and fulfilling life.
