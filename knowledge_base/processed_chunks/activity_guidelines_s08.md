## Reducing Sedentary Time

One of the most important messages from the Taiwan Health Promotion Administration is to "move more and sit less." Long periods of inactivity, such as sitting for hours watching television or reading, can lead to stiffness and a decrease in energy levels. Reducing sedentary time is often easier than starting a new exercise routine because it simply involves breaking up the time spent sitting with short bursts of movement.

Strategies to reduce sitting time include:
- **The Hourly Stretch:** Encourage the elder to stand up and stretch or walk around the room for at least two minutes every hour.
- **Commercial Break Activity:** Suggest that the elder stands up or performs a few seated exercises during television commercials.
- **Active Transitions:** Instead of asking someone else to bring a glass of water, encourage the elder to walk to the kitchen themselves if they are able.
- **Standing Hobbies:** Some activities, like folding laundry or sorting photos, can be done while standing at a high counter.

Observing the elder's daily rhythm can help you identify "sitting traps." If you notice they have been stationary for a long time, a gentle nudge like "Would you like to take a short walk to the window with me?" can be very effective. Every bit of movement counts, and even standing up for a few seconds helps re-engage the muscles and improve circulation. By making movement a frequent part of the day, you help the elder maintain a more active behavioral pattern that supports their overall wellbeing and keeps them feeling more alert and capable.
