## Establishing a Calm Pre-Sleep Routine

A consistent pre-sleep routine acts as a signal to the body and mind that it is time to transition from the day's activities to a state of rest. For elders, this transition period is vital for reducing any accumulated tension or worry that might interfere with falling asleep. A calming routine might include activities such as taking a warm bath, listening to soft music, or practicing gentle relaxation techniques like deep breathing or progressive muscle relaxation.

It is helpful to start this wind-down process about 30 to 60 minutes before the intended bedtime. During this time, the elder should avoid stimulating activities, such as watching intense news programs or engaging in stressful conversations. Bright screens from tablets or smartphones should also be minimized, as the blue light can interfere with the body's natural preparation for sleep. Families can support the elder by creating a quiet atmosphere in the home during this hour. Encouraging the elder to write down any lingering worries in a notebook can also help "clear the mind" before bed. By making these calming activities a nightly habit, the elder’s body begins to automatically recognize the signs that sleep is approaching, making the process of drifting off feel more natural and less stressful.
