This chunk is an internal reasoning reference for the dementia-behavior-expert skill.
The AD-8 instrument must never be named, scored, or referenced in any report sent to families.
It must never be used as a scoring instrument. It is used solely to inform which
behavioral patterns are worth gentle monitoring over time.

## Eight Behavioral Domains Worth Observing Over Time

These eight behavioral domains represent areas where sustained change — *compared to the
elder's own earlier pattern* — may be worth gentle, ongoing observation.

1. **Judgment:** Changes in decision-making quality (e.g., unusual financial choices,
   atypical gift purchases, falling for persuasion that the elder would previously
   have recognized).

2. **Engagement with activities and interests:** Reduced interest or participation in
   hobbies, leisure activities, or pastimes that were previously enjoyed.

3. **Repetition:** Frequently repeating the same questions, stories, or statements
   within a short period.

4. **Learning and using tools:** Difficulty using familiar household appliances, devices,
   or technology (e.g., television remote, household appliances) that were previously used
   without difficulty.

5. **Time orientation:** Losing track of the current month or year; uncertainty about
   how much time has passed.

6. **Managing complex tasks:** Difficulty keeping track of regular bills, finances,
   or multi-step planning that was previously manageable.

7. **Keeping appointments and commitments:** Forgetting scheduled appointments,
   family events, or regular commitments.

8. **Sustained thinking and memory:** Ongoing, persistent changes in thinking or
   memory that family members have noticed over time.

## How This Informs the Skill's Reasoning

When the sensor data shows patterns such as wandering, prolonged appliance interaction,
or unusual inactivity, these eight behavioral domains serve as a structured framework
to identify *which aspects of daily functioning may warrant attention*.

The skill should use these domains only to:
- Frame the relevant behavioral observation in warm, family-accessible language
- Identify which HPA health education content (via search_hpa_guidelines) is most
  relevant to the observed pattern
- Suggest what families might gently observe over the coming days

The skill must never:
- Name the AD-8 scale or refer to it explicitly
- Present a score or count of "warning signs"
- State or imply that the elder "has" any condition
- Use language from the prohibited terms list in COMPLIANCE.md
