## Selecting Proper Footwear and Clothing

What an elder wears can significantly impact their stability. You might consider choosing shoes that fit snugly, have low or flat heels, and feature non-slip soles for better traction. Shoes with velcro straps or laces are often safer than slip-ons because they provide more secure support for the foot. Avoid loose-fitting slippers or walking in socks on smooth floors, as these can easily lead to a slip. Clothing should also be considered; trousers that are too long can be a major tripping hazard. You might suggest hemline adjustments to ensure that pants do not drag on the floor. If you observe the elder shuffling their feet or appearing to trip over their own footwear, it may be time to review their shoe size and condition. Well-maintained shoes with good grip help the elder feel more grounded and secure during their daily movements.
