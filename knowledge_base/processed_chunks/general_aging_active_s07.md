## Creating a Safe Environment for Movement

A safe home environment is essential for encouraging the elder to move confidently. Families should ensure that floors are flat and free of clutter, such as loose rugs or electrical cords, which could cause a trip. Lighting should be bright and accessible, especially in hallways and bathrooms. When the elder is performing strengthening activities, they should have access to sturdy furniture, like a heavy chair or a stable countertop, to use for balance support. Proper footwear is also crucial; the elder should wear well-fitting, flat-bottomed shoes even when indoors. By making these environmental adjustments, the family creates a space where the elder feels empowered to stay active without fear of falling.
