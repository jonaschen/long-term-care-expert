## Bedroom Safety and Layout

A safe bedroom environment supports the elder's independence, especially during transitions in and out of bed. Ensure the bed is at an appropriate height—ideally between 45 to 50 centimeters—so that the elder’s feet can rest flat on the floor while they are sitting on the edge. You might consider removing all clutter from the path between the bed and the door, and from the bed to the bathroom. Tucking away electrical cords and removing small throw rugs can significantly reduce tripping hazards. If the elder seems to struggle with finding their way at night, a motion-sensor nightlight near the bedside can provide immediate visibility. Keeping a sturdy chair in the room can also provide a safe place for the elder to sit while dressing. If you observe a behavioral pattern where the elder frequently holds onto furniture while moving across the room, it suggests that the walking path should be widened or more permanent handrails should be installed.
