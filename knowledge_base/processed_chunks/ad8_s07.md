## Consistency in Keeping Track of Appointments

This domain focuses on whether the elder is having more trouble remembering scheduled events, commitments, or social dates compared to their past habits. It’s about the consistency of their memory for future plans and time-based responsibilities.

Concrete daily-life examples:
- Missing a long-standing weekly appointment, such as a regular meeting with a friend at a park or a scheduled hair salon visit that they have kept for years.
- Forgetting a major family event, like a grandchild's birthday or a wedding anniversary, that they have always celebrated and remembered in the past.
- Showing up for a meeting at a community center on the wrong day, or perhaps arriving several hours too early or too late.
- Frequently asking family members "What am I supposed to do today?" or "Do I have anywhere to go?" despite having a clear and visible schedule.

How to gently observe:
Use a shared family calendar or a large, visible wall calendar in a common area. Mention an upcoming event a day or two in advance and see if they remember it the following morning or afternoon. Observe if they naturally refer to the calendar for guidance, or if they seem genuinely surprised that the event was even planned.
