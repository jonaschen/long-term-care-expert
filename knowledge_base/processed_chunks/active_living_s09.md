## Seated Exercises for Limited Mobility

For elders who find standing for long periods difficult, many beneficial movements can be done while seated. A simple yet effective exercise is the seated leg extension. While sitting firmly in a chair, the elder lifts one foot and straightens the knee, holding the position for 5 to 20 seconds before slowly lowering it. This can be repeated with the other leg.

Reaching movements can also be done from a chair. The elder can practice reaching forward, upward, or to the sides to grab imaginary items, which helps with upper body flexibility and core engagement. These exercises can even be done while watching television or waiting for a bus. If the elder seems to slide forward in the chair, remind them to keep their hips all the way back against the chair's backrest for better support. Seated activities provide a safe way for those with limited mobility to maintain their muscle tone and joint health.
