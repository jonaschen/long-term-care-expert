## Challenges with New Technology and Appliances

This area looks at whether the elder is struggling to learn how to operate devices, tools, or small appliances that should be within their capability to understand. It highlights a change in the ability to follow new instructions or adapt to minor changes in their environment.

Concrete daily-life examples:
- Frequent frustration when trying to use a new television remote control, even if it has similar buttons to the old one they used for years.
- Difficulty remembering the basic steps to operate a new microwave, washing machine, or water heater after multiple explanations.
- Struggle to navigate a basic smartphone application, like a messaging app, that was previously explained and that they had expressed interest in using.
- Confusion when trying to use a new kitchen gadget, like an electric kettle or a blender, despite having used similar tools throughout their adult life.

How to gently observe:
Introduce a very simple new item or a minor change to an existing setup, such as a new brand of a familiar cleaning tool. Offer to show them how it works in a relaxed way, and then watch how they attempt to use it a few hours later. See if they can follow the basic steps independently or if the newness of the object causes significant confusion or distress.
