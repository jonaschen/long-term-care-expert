## Stairway Safety and Visual Contrast

Staircases are areas where a fall can be particularly serious, so extra precautions are necessary. You might consider ensuring that handrails are installed on both sides of every staircase and that they extend slightly beyond the top and bottom steps. Good lighting at both the top and bottom of the stairs is essential to avoid shadows that can hide steps. Using high-contrast anti-slip strips on the edge of each step can help the elder clearly distinguish where one step ends and the next begins. If the elder seems to hesitate at the top of the stairs or grips the handrail very tightly, it suggests that better lighting or more visible step edges are needed. Ensure the stairs are never used for temporary storage of items. If you observe the elder having more difficulty with stairs than usual, it may be a sign to prioritize more activities on the ground floor.
