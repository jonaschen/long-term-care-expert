## Priority Home Modifications and Grab Bars

When planning home modifications, it is helpful to prioritize changes that address the areas of highest risk and most frequent use. Installing professional-grade grab bars in the bathroom—specifically near the toilet and inside the shower—is often the single most impactful adjustment. These bars provide a secure, heavy-duty point of contact that a simple towel rack or door handle cannot offer. You might consider choosing bars with a textured surface for a better grip, even when hands are wet.

Another priority modification is the removal of floor thresholds between rooms. If removal is not possible, installing low-profile transition strips or small ramps can create a smoother surface for walking and the use of mobility aids. You might also consider applying high-contrast anti-slip tape to the edges of any steps or small level changes within the home. This visual marker helps the elder perceive the change in depth more clearly, reducing the risk of a misstep.

It is helpful to ensure that all modifications are performed by a professional to guarantee they can support the elder's weight safely. Compared to temporary solutions like loose mats or makeshift supports, these permanent environmental changes provide a much higher level of security. If you observe the elder frequently touching walls or leaning on furniture for support, it is a clear indicator that a strategic modification, such as an additional handrail in a hallway, would be a valuable investment in their safety.
