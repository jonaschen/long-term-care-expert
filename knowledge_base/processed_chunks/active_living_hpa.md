## Core Idea: Movement Woven Into Daily Life

The goal is not a separate "exercise session" — it is strengthening balance, muscle strength, flexibility, and cardiovascular endurance through activities the elder already does every day.

## Five Everyday Movement Opportunities

- **While watching TV:** Place the remote on a higher shelf to encourage reaching and stretching. Practice gentle half-squats or extend one leg straight while seated to train the lower limbs.
- **While eating:** Place a dish slightly farther away and reach for it, holding the stretch for 10–30 seconds.
- **While shopping:** Carry a light shopping bag to train hand muscles and core stability.
- **While waiting (e.g., for a bus):** Stand with feet together and rise onto tiptoes to build balance and core strength.
- **While doing household chores:** Light activities (washing dishes, watering plants) and moderate activities (mopping, cooking) all count as physical activity.

## Recommended Intensity and Frequency

- **Target:** Accumulate 150 minutes of moderate-intensity activity per week, in sessions of at least 10 minutes each.
- **Safety:** Ensure good lighting, non-slip surfaces, flat-soled shoes, and adequate hydration during activity.
- **Key message:** Reduce long periods of sitting — sitting for more than 4 continuous hours increases cardiovascular risk.
