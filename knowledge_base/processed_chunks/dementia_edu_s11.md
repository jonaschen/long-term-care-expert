## Personal Care Assistance

Assisting an elder with personal care, such as bathing or dressing, requires sensitivity and a focus on maintaining their dignity. You might observe that the elder becomes resistant to these tasks, perhaps because they feel vulnerable or confused by the process. To make personal care more comfortable, you might consider establishing a consistent routine so the elder knows what to expect. For example, bathing at the same time and in the same way each day can reduce anxiety.

Communication is essential during these moments. Explain each step clearly and gently before you do it, such as saying, 'Now I am going to help you put on your sweater.' Whenever possible, encourage the elder to do as much as they can for themselves, even if it is just holding a washcloth or choosing between two shirts. This helps them maintain a sense of independence and control. Ensure the bathroom is warm and well-lit, and use safety features like non-slip mats and grab bars. If the elder becomes very upset, it may be better to stop and try again later rather than causing further distress. Respecting their privacy and personal space while providing the necessary support shows that you value them as an individual, making these daily tasks a time of gentle care rather than conflict.
