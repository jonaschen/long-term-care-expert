## Talking with Other Family Members

Supporting an elder is often most successful when it is a shared effort among family members. You might consider holding regular family meetings to discuss the elder's current needs and to divide caregiving tasks. This prevents any one person from becoming overwhelmed and ensures that everyone is updated on any changes in the elder's behavioral patterns. Be open and honest about the challenges you are facing, and encourage others to contribute in ways that match their own strengths and schedules.

It is also important to talk about future planning together. You might consider having a calm discussion about how to manage the elder's assets or what kind of long-term support they would prefer. If some family members find it difficult to understand the changes they see in the elder, you might share educational resources or invite them to spend time observing the daily routine. This builds a common understanding and fosters a more supportive environment for both the elder and the primary caregiver. By working together as a team, you provide a stronger safety net for your loved one and ensure that the caregiving journey is sustainable for the entire family.
