## Nutrition: The Healthy Plate
- **Balanced Diet:** Follow the "palm-sized protein, fist-sized fruit" and "more vegetables than fruit" rule.
- **Three-Highs Control:** Avoid high-salt/fat foods. Prioritize whole foods.
- **Protein:** Increase intake (e.g., soy milk, eggs) to maintain muscle strength, adjusting texture for dental health.

## Exercise: Strength and Balance
- **Aerobic:** Aim for 150 minutes of moderate intensity per week (walking, swimming).
- **Resistance:** Push-ups against walls, water bottle lifting, or seated leg raises.
- **Balance:** Practice balance exercises regularly to reduce fall risk.

## Daily Routine and Self-Care
- **Regularity:** Maintaining consistent meal times, activity schedules, and sleep routines supports overall wellbeing.
- **Social Engagement:** Participate in community centers or seniors' schools to stay mentally active and socially connected.

## Social and Mental Health
- **Engagement:** Participate in community centers or seniors' schools to stimulate the brain and reduce stress.
- **Emotion:** Low mood may be related to chronic conditions; seek professional evaluation rather than self-labeling as "depressed."
