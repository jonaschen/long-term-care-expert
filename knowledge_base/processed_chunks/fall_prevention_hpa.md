## Core Strategy: The Three Steps
1. **Self-Management:** Regular exercise to strengthen muscle and balance. If the elder experiences dizziness, consult a professional to review any factors that may contribute.
2. **Environment Improvement:** Ensure adequate lighting, clear pathways, and non-slip mats in bathrooms.
3. **Outdoor Safety:** Wear well-fitting clothes and non-slip shoes. Use assistive devices like canes if needed.

## The Five Skills Secret
- **Nutrition:** Adequate protein and calories to prevent frailty.
- **Exercise:** Practice strength, flexibility, and balance (e.g., Tai Chi, single-leg stands).
- **Home Modification:** Install grab bars in bathrooms, use nightlights, and remove floor clutter.
- **Health Review:** If the elder is seeing multiple specialists, ask their primary care doctor to review all health factors together to reduce fall risk.
- **Apparel:** Choose shoes with laces or velcro; avoid slippers or long trousers.

## Observation Tips
- **Rising:** Sit at the bedside for a moment after waking to avoid postural hypotension.
- **Visual Cues:** Use high-contrast anti-slip strips on stair edges for better visibility.
