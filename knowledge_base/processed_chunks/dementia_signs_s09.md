## Decreased Engagement in Social Life

You might observe the elder becoming more passive or showing less interest in hobbies, social gatherings, or activities they used to love. This is often more than just a temporary lack of energy; it can be a long-term withdrawal. They may spend a lot of time sitting quietly or sleeping more during the day, and they might need constant encouragement to participate in family events.

**Daily Life Example:** An elder who used to enjoy going to the park to chat with neighbors might now prefer to sit in front of the television for hours on end, even if they aren't truly watching the program. They might stop initiating conversations and only respond with short, one-word answers when prompted.

**Gentle Supportive Action:** Invite the elder to participate in simple, low-pressure activities that align with their past interests. Instead of asking, "Do you want to go out?" which they might refuse, try saying, "I would love your company for a short walk to see the flowers." Focus on the enjoyment of the moment rather than the completion of a specific goal.
