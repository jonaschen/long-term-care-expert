## Rising Safely from Chairs and Beds

Sudden changes in posture can sometimes lead to temporary instability. To assist the elder, you might suggest a gradual rising routine, often called the "sit and wait" method. When waking up, the elder should lie still for a minute, then sit at the bedside for another minute with their feet flat on the floor, and finally stand steadily for a minute before they start to walk. This allows their body to adjust to the change in position. When rising from a chair, they should lean forward slightly and use the armrests for support. If the elder seems to experience a moment of hesitation or swaying when they first stand up, encouraging this slow sequence can be very helpful. If you observe that the elder frequently reaches for support immediately after standing, it is a prompt to ensure a sturdy piece of furniture or a grab bar is within easy reach of their favorite seating area.
