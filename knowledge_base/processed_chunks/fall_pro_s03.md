## Bedroom Safety and Bed Height

Ensuring a safe bedroom environment is critical for preventing falls, particularly during the transitions of waking up and going to bed. One of the most important environmental factors is the height of the bed. Ideally, the bed should be approximately 45 to 50 centimeters high, allowing the elder's feet to rest flat on the floor while they are sitting on the edge. If the bed is too high, the elder may struggle to reach the floor, while a bed that is too low can make rising more difficult and strenuous. 

You might consider organizing the bedroom so that frequently used items, such as glasses, a telephone, or a water bottle, are kept on a sturdy nightstand within easy reach. This prevents the elder from having to lean or stretch excessively while in bed. The path from the bed to the bathroom should be kept entirely clear of furniture and clutter. 

Lighting plays a significant role in nighttime safety. You might consider placing a lamp within reach of the bed and installing motion-sensor nightlights along the path to the bathroom. If you observe the elder appearing disoriented or hesitant when they wake up at night, ensure the lighting is even and free of deep shadows. Providing a stable chair in the bedroom can also be helpful, giving the elder a place to sit comfortably while dressing. By focusing on these specific adjustments, you create a supportive space that respects the elder's rhythm and promotes a safer start and end to their day.
