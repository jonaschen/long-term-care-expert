## Signs That an Elder's Needs Are Changing — When to Gently Seek Advice

Families are often the first to notice when an elder's needs might be shifting. The key is to look for persistent changes in the elder's *own usual pattern* rather than comparing them to others. A single instance of forgetfulness or a one-time stumble is rarely a cause for concern, but a pattern of behavioral shifts that continues over several days or weeks is worth observing more closely.

Behavioral signs that might indicate a change in needs include:
- **Social Withdrawal:** The elder seems noticeably less interested in hobbies or social gatherings they used to enjoy.
- **Unusual Fatigue:** They seem to be much more tired than usual after performing their typical daily routines.
- **Changes in Movement:** You notice they are 'furniture walking' (using walls and chairs for support) more frequently or their gait seems noticeably less steady than it was a month ago.
- **Daily Routine Shifts:** They are taking much longer to complete familiar tasks or seem uncertain about steps in a routine they have always known.

If you observe these persistent changes, the most supportive next step is a gentle one. You might suggest accompanying the elder to a family medicine clinic or a community health center for a general 'wellbeing review.' Frame this as a way to ensure they are getting the best support for their active lifestyle. By noticing these signs early and seeking professional advice in a low-pressure way, you help ensure that the elder continues to receive the right environment and encouragement for their changing needs.
