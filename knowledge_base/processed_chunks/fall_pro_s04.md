## Living Room and Pathway Maintenance

The living room is often the center of daily activity, making it essential to maintain a layout that supports safe movement. You might consider arranging furniture to provide wide, unobstructed paths that accommodate the use of a cane or walker if needed. It is helpful to ensure that all rugs are either removed or firmly secured to the floor using high-quality double-sided tape or specialized non-slip backing. Loose rug edges are a common cause of stumbles and should be addressed immediately.

Observe the elder's favorite seating. A sturdy chair with firm armrests is generally better than a soft, low sofa, as the armrests provide essential support when the elder is sitting down or standing up. If the chair seems too low, you might consider using a firm cushion to raise the seat height, ensuring their knees remain at a 90-degree angle. 

Compared to the usual pattern of having multiple small tables or decorative items in the center of the room, keeping the middle of the living space clear allows for more confident walking. Ensure that lighting is bright and consistent across the entire room to avoid dark corners. If you observe the elder 'furniture walking'—touching the backs of chairs or walls for support—it suggests that the pathways might be too wide or that they would benefit from more frequent rest stops or a walking aid. Regular maintenance of these pathways ensures that the elder can move with greater security and ease throughout their day.
