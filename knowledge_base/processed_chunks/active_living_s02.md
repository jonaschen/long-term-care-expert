## Creating a Safe Home Environment for Activity

Before starting any movement routine at home, it is important to ensure the surroundings are safe. The floor should be flat, dry, and free of clutter or loose rugs that could cause a trip. Ensure the lighting is bright enough to clearly see all pathways. 

Household items can often be repurposed as support tools. For example, a sturdy chair without wheels or a heavy, stable table can be used for balance support during standing exercises. When performing movements that require more space, such as walking in patterns, ensure there is a clear path at least several meters long. If the elder seems to struggle with space at home, you might consider moving some furniture to create a dedicated 'activity zone'. A safe environment reduces the risk of sudden drops or falls, allowing the elder to focus on the quality of their movements.
