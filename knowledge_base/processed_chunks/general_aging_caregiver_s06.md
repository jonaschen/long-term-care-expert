## Managing the Emotional Weight of Watching an Elder Change

One of the most difficult aspects of caregiving is witnessing the gradual change in an elder's personality, memory, or behavior. It is natural to feel a sense of grief for the person they used to be, even while they are still with you. You might find yourself feeling sad, frustrated, or even angry when the elder forgets a significant event or struggles with a task they once did with ease. Acknowledging this 'anticipatory grief' is an important part of the caregiving process. It allows you to process your emotions rather than suppressing them, which can eventually lead to burnout.

To manage this emotional weight, try to focus on the connection you have with the elder in the present moment. While their factual memory may change, their emotional memory—how they feel when you are near—often remains very strong. You might consider looking at old photo albums together or listening to music from their youth, focusing on the shared feeling of the activity rather than the accuracy of the memories. If the elder expresses confusion or says something that seems unusual, try to respond to the underlying emotion rather than correcting the details. By shifting your focus from 'what has been lost' to 'what remains,' you can find moments of genuine connection that provide emotional sustenance for both you and the elder.
