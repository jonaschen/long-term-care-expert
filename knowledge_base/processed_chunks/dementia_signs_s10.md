## Observing Shifts in Personal Temperament

You might notice significant changes in the elder's personality or the way they interact with others. A person who was once very outgoing might become withdrawn, or someone who was typically calm might become irritable or suspicious. They may also develop a strong dependency on one specific family member or show signs of anxiety in unfamiliar situations.

**Daily Life Example:** The elder might become uncharacteristically suspicious of neighbors, believing people are talking about them. They might also become very fearful of leaving the house or insist on following a family member from room to room (often called "shadowing") because they feel insecure when that person is out of sight.

**Gentle Supportive Action:** Provide a consistent and predictable daily schedule, as routine offers a sense of security. Reassure the elder frequently with kind words and physical presence. If they are becoming overly dependent on one person, try to involve other family members in small, positive ways to gradually broaden the elder's circle of comfort without causing them distress.
