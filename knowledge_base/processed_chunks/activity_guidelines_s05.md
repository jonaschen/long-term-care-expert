## Activity for Elders with Limited Mobility

Even when an elder experiences changes in mobility or finds it difficult to stand for long periods, staying active remains incredibly important. Movement can be adapted to fit any capability level, and seated or supported exercises are highly effective for maintaining strength and circulation. The focus should be on what is possible within the elder's current comfort zone, ensuring that they continue to engage their body every day.

Suggested activities for limited mobility include:
- **Seated Leg Lifts:** While sitting in a sturdy chair, the elder can slowly straighten one leg, hold it for a few seconds, and then lower it. This helps maintain the strength needed for standing up.
- **Arm Circles and Reaching:** Gentle arm movements while seated can improve shoulder flexibility and help the elder continue to reach for items on shelves safely.
- **Seated Marches:** "Walking" while sitting by lifting the knees one at a time helps with hip mobility and circulation.
- **Supported Standing:** Using a stable surface like a heavy dining table or a kitchen counter for support while performing gentle side-steps or calf raises.

When supporting an elder with limited mobility, it is vital to create a safe environment. Ensure the chair they use is stable and has armrests if needed. Observe their behavioral patterns during these activities—if they seem hesitant, offer encouragement or a steady hand. You might consider integrating these movements into their television time or while they are waiting for a meal. By keeping the body moving in even small ways, the elder can preserve their current level of function and potentially improve their confidence in movement over time.
