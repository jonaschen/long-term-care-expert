## Taking Breaks Without Guilt — Respite Planning for Families

Many family caregivers feel a sense of guilt when they think about taking time for themselves, fearing that no one else can provide the same level of care for the elder. However, taking regular breaks is not an act of selfishness; it is a vital strategy for preventing exhaustion and maintaining the quality of your interactions. Respite care allows you to step away for a few hours or days to rest, attend to your own health, or simply reconnect with your personal life. This 'relay' approach ensures that the elder receives consistent support while you have the opportunity to recharge.

In Taiwan, the **1966 Long-term Care Hotline** is an essential resource for families to learn about available respite services. These services can include professional helpers coming to your home for a few hours or the elder spending time at a local day care center. Using these resources allows the elder to experience a different social environment, which can sometimes be a helpful change in their daily rhythm. Planning your breaks in advance—rather than waiting until you are at a breaking point—helps create a stable routine for the elder and a predictable rest schedule for you. By prioritizing these intervals of rest, you are ensuring that you can continue to support the elder with warmth and patience over the long term.
