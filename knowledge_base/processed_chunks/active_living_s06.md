## Neck and Shoulder Flexibility Stretches

Maintaining mobility in the neck and shoulders can help the elder perform daily tasks more easily. Simple stretches can be integrated into the day. One effective movement involves reaching both hands upward toward the sky, as if greeting the morning sun. This helps stretch the shoulder joints and upper body muscles.

Another useful activity is reaching for items on high shelves. The elder can practice this by reaching as high as they can and holding the position for 10 to 30 seconds. If the elder experiences shoulder discomfort, they should avoid forcing the movement and keep the reach within a comfortable range. You might consider placing frequently used items slightly higher than usual to encourage these stretches naturally. Regular shoulder movements help prevent the feeling of stiffness and support the elder's ability to dress themselves or reach for items independently.
