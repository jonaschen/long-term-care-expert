## Disorientation in Time and Space

The elder might seem to lose their sense of time or become confused about where they are. While most people might occasionally forget the date, the elder might lose track of the season or even the year. More significantly, they may become disoriented in places that should be very familiar to them, such as their own neighborhood or the route to a local park they visit daily.

**Daily Life Example:** The elder might walk out to the corner store and suddenly forget which direction leads back to their front door. They might also become confused about time, such as preparing for a morning activity in the middle of the night because they saw the light from a streetlamp and thought it was sunrise.

**Gentle Supportive Action:** To help the elder stay oriented, place a large digital clock that clearly displays the time, day of the week, and whether it is "Morning" or "Evening." For physical orientation, you might consider placing a familiar landmark, such as a brightly colored flowerpot or a specific piece of outdoor decor, near the entrance of the home to make it more easily recognizable from the street.
