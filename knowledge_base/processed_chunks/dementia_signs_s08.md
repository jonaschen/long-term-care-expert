## Shifts in Emotional Expression

The elder's mood might seem more unpredictable than in the past. You may observe sudden shifts in emotion that do not seem to have a clear external cause. They might become easily upset, tearful, or angry, even in a calm environment. These emotional changes are often a reflection of the elder's internal confusion or frustration with their surroundings.

**Daily Life Example:** The elder might be enjoying a peaceful afternoon and then suddenly become very agitated or angry because a familiar routine was slightly changed, such as lunch being served ten minutes later than usual. Their moods may fluctuate rapidly, swinging from happy to sad without a clear explanation.

**Gentle Supportive Action:** When you notice a sudden shift in mood, maintain a calm, soothing tone of voice. Avoid arguing or trying to use logic to talk them out of their feelings. Instead, try to provide a comforting distraction, such as playing some of their favorite music or offering a light snack. Validating their feelings with a simple, "I can see you're upset, let's sit together for a moment," can often help de-escalate the situation.
