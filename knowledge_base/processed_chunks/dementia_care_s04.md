## Mealtime Strategies

Maintaining good nutrition is vital for an elder's health, but changes in memory can sometimes make mealtimes challenging. You might observe that the elder becomes easily distracted, has difficulty using utensils, or forgets to eat. To support them, consider creating a calm, quiet dining environment with minimal background noise. Using a plain-colored tablecloth and plates that contrast with the color of the food can help the elder see their meal more clearly.

If using traditional utensils becomes difficult, you might consider serving 'finger foods' like sliced fruit, small sandwiches, or steamed vegetable sticks that are easy to pick up. Offering smaller, more frequent meals throughout the day can also be more manageable than three large ones. During the meal, you can provide gentle prompts or model the action of eating to help the elder get started. It is also important to observe their swallowing patterns; if they seem to have difficulty, you might consider adjusting the texture of the food to make it softer. Encourage them to drink plenty of fluids, as hydration is essential for physical and mental wellbeing. By making mealtimes relaxed and social, you can help the elder maintain their appetite and enjoy the experience of sharing a meal with loved ones.
