## Monitoring Changes in Energy and Engagement

Families can gently monitor an elder's wellbeing by observing how they handle daily tasks. If chores like bathing, grocery shopping, or walking to the bus station seem to be becoming more "strenuous" or "exhausting," it may indicate a change in their energy levels. Using a simple weekly checklist to record the duration and type of activities the elder performs can help identify trends. For instance, if the elder used to enjoy a 20-minute walk but now prefers to stop after five minutes, this is a meaningful observation. This gentle monitoring is not about restriction but about understanding the elder's evolving needs so that the family can provide the right level of support and encouragement.
