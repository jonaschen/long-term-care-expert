## Nutrition and Hydration Support

Proper nutrition and hydration are essential for the physical and mental wellbeing of the elder. You might observe changes in their eating patterns, such as appearing to forget to eat or showing a preference for only certain types of food. To support their intake, you might consider using transparent cups, which make water and other beverages more visible and inviting. Providing small, frequent meals rather than three large ones can also make eating feel less like a chore and more like a series of light activities throughout the day.

Observe the elder's cues during mealtimes. If they seem to lose interest in a meal, offering a variety of colors and textures might help stimulate their appetite. It is also helpful to keep healthy snacks easily accessible in visible locations. Ensure that the dining area is well-lit and free from distractions like a loud television, which can make it hard for the elder to focus on the task of eating. If you observe the elder struggling with traditional drinking glasses, consider using cups with large handles or straws. By focusing on these small environmental adjustments and maintaining a close observation of their habits, you can help ensure the elder remains well-nourished and hydrated, which supports their overall vitality and mood.
