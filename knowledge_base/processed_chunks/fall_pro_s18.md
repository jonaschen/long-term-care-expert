## When to Consult a Professional for Mobility

While many fall prevention strategies are lifestyle and environment-based, it is important to know when a behavioral pattern suggests the need for a professional wellness review. You might consider keeping a simple log of the elder's mobility. If you observe a persistent pattern where they have significantly more difficulty walking than usual, or if their pace has slowed down noticeably compared to their usual pattern, it is helpful to discuss these observations with a professional.

Frequent near-misses or a new, expressed fear of falling are also important indicators that the current home environment or activity level may need a more detailed review. If the elder seems unusually tired, dizzy, or if they experience a sudden change in their ability to perform daily tasks like rising from a chair, a general health check is recommended. These patterns can sometimes be related to simple lifestyle factors that a professional can help identify and address.

When consulting a professional, it is helpful to share your specific observations about the elder's daily movement and any environmental changes you have made. This collaborative approach ensures that any further suggestions are well-informed by the elder's actual behavioral patterns. By staying observant and acting when you notice persistent changes, you provide a proactive level of care that supports the elder's long-term safety and independence at home.
