## Setting Realistic Expectations and Celebrating Small Wins

In a caregiving role, it is easy to fall into the trap of setting unreachable goals, such as trying to 'fix' the elder's behavior or expecting them to return to their previous level of independence. When these expectations aren't met, it can lead to feelings of failure and frustration for both you and the elder. Instead, a more sustainable approach is to focus on maintaining a high quality of life and a sense of calm in the home. This means adjusting your expectations to match the elder's current abilities and recognizing that their 'best' may look different from day to day.

Learning to celebrate 'small wins' can significantly change the atmosphere of your home. A small win might be a day where the elder's mealtime went smoothly, a moment of shared laughter, or an afternoon where they seemed particularly relaxed. These moments may seem minor, but they are evidence of your successful efforts to create a supportive environment. When you notice that the elder has completed a simple task or expressed a moment of clarity, take a second to acknowledge it. By focusing on these positive intervals, you move away from a mindset of constant problem-solving and toward one of appreciation for the present. This shift in perspective helps reduce the pressure on you and allows the elder to feel more successful and less anxious in their daily life.
