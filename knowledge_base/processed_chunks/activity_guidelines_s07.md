## Muscle Strengthening Recommendations

Muscle strength is the "engine" that allows an elder to move through their world. As patterns of movement change with age, keeping the muscles engaged helps support the joints and maintains the power needed for essential activities like climbing stairs or getting out of bed. Strengthening activities should be performed at least twice a week, focusing on all the major muscle groups, including the legs, back, abdomen, and arms.

Practical ways to strengthen muscles include:
- **Bodyweight Squats:** Slowly sitting down into a chair and then standing back up without using hands, if possible. This is one of the best exercises for lower body power.
- **Resistance Bands:** Using simple elastic bands to perform rows or bicep curls, which provides a gentle way to challenge the muscles.
- **Wall Push-ups:** Standing a few feet from a wall and performing a push-up motion against it to support chest and arm strength.
- **Lifting Light Loads:** Using small water bottles or light cans as weights for arm exercises.

It is important to focus on repetitions rather than heavy weight. Performing a movement 10 to 15 times until the muscle feels slightly tired is a good target. Observe the elder's form—movements should be slow and controlled, avoiding any sudden jerking. If an elder is new to strengthening, you might consider starting with just one set of a few exercises and gradually increasing as they feel more comfortable. Strengthening muscles is a long-term strategy for maintaining the ability to live independently and perform daily tasks with ease.
