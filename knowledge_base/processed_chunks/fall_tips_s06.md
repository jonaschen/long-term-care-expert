## Lighting Improvements for Better Visibility

Good lighting is one of the most effective ways to prevent falls by helping the elder identify obstacles and depth changes. You might consider installing high-wattage bulbs in all rooms and ensuring that light switches are easily accessible at the entrance of every room. For nighttime safety, motion-sensor lights or glowing toggle switches can guide the elder without them having to search for a switch in the dark. It is also helpful to apply high-contrast anti-slip strips to the edges of all stairs, making each step more visible. If the elder seems to squint or move more cautiously in dim areas, it indicates that the current lighting level is insufficient. Regularly checking and replacing burnt-out bulbs ensures that the home remains a consistently safe environment. Bright, even lighting without harsh glare helps the elder maintain their orientation and balance while moving.
