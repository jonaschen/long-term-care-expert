## Moderate Activity Examples (Active Living)

Moderate-intensity activity is a key component of a healthy lifestyle for elders. These activities are defined as movements that cause a noticeable increase in breathing and heart rate, but the elder should still be able to speak comfortably in full sentences. This level of effort is often described as "feeling a bit warm" or "breathing a little faster than usual." Integrating moderate activity into the week helps support heart health and overall stamina.

Practical examples of moderate activity for elders include:
- **Brisk walking:** Walking as if you are slightly in a hurry to catch a bus or meet a friend.
- **Active chores:** Mopping the floors, vacuuming, or scrubbing surfaces require more effort and engage more muscle groups than light dusting.
- **Outdoor work:** Gentle gardening, such as weeding or planting, and carrying light groceries (around 5kg) are excellent moderate tasks.
- **Social movement:** Activities like slow ballroom dancing, water aerobics, or low-impact community exercise classes.

Families can encourage these activities by making them part of a shared routine. For instance, a "brisk walk" to a local park or market can become a pleasant daily habit. It is important to observe the elder’s response to these activities. They should feel challenged but not exhausted. If the elder seems to be struggling to catch their breath or appears overly fatigued compared to their usual pattern, it is a sign to slow down or take a break. The goal is to find a pace that feels sustainable and enjoyable, turning movement into a rewarding part of their active living strategy.
