## Managing the Shift in Internal Clocks

A common observation in older adults is a shift in the circadian rhythm, which is the body's internal 24-hour clock. This shift often causes elders to feel tired much earlier in the evening—perhaps as early as 7:00 PM or 8:00 PM—and consequently wake up very early in the morning, such as 4:00 AM or 5:00 AM. This pattern is a normal physiological change and does not necessarily indicate a problem that requires professional intervention.

If the elder finds this early-waking pattern distressing or if it interferes with family life, there are environmental ways to help gently adjust the clock. Increasing exposure to bright, natural sunlight in the late afternoon can help signal to the body that it is not yet time for sleep. Conversely, keeping the home well-lit in the early evening may delay the onset of sleepiness. It is generally not recommended to use artificial aids to force the elder to stay awake later or sleep longer, as this can disrupt the natural sleep structure. Instead, families can help the elder find meaningful early-morning activities, such as light stretching or reading, to make use of their natural wakefulness. Recognizing this shift as a natural adjustment rather than a concern can reduce anxiety for both the elder and the caregiver.
