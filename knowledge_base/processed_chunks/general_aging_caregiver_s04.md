## Maintaining Your Own Health While Caring for an Elder

It is often said that you cannot pour from an empty cup. As a caregiver, your physical and mental health are your most important tools. When the elder's needs become more demanding, your own health habits—such as eating regular meals, staying active, and getting enough rest—are often the first things to be sacrificed. Over time, this can lead to your own health challenges, making it even harder to provide the care the elder requires. You might consider setting a 'non-negotiable' health goal for yourself, such as a 20-minute walk in the morning or ensuring you have a quiet meal away from distractions.

Pay attention to your own physical signals, such as tension in your shoulders or changes in your appetite. Regular exercise, even in small amounts, can be a powerful way to manage the stress that often accompanies caregiving. Additionally, keeping up with your own medical check-ups and screenings is essential. If the elder's nighttime patterns are disrupting your sleep, it is important to address this by looking into environmental adjustments or seeking advice from the **0800-474-580** caregiver support line. By consciously investing in your own health, you are not taking away from the elder; instead, you are building the stamina and resilience needed to be a calm and steady presence in their life.
