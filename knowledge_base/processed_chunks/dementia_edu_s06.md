## Responding to Repetitive Questions

It is common to observe an elder asking the same question or performing the same action multiple times within a short period. This often happens because the elder is seeking reassurance or has lost track of the immediate past. When faced with repetitive behavior, you might consider the emotional need behind the question. For example, if they repeatedly ask when a family member is coming to visit, they may be feeling lonely or anxious. Providing a calm, brief answer each time, without pointing out that they have already asked, helps maintain their dignity.

Using visual aids can also be very effective. If the elder asks about the time or the next meal, a visible clock or a daily schedule on a whiteboard can provide the answer they need without requiring them to ask. Sometimes, a gentle distraction or redirection can help break the cycle of repetition. You might suggest a simple activity they enjoy, such as listening to a favorite song or helping with a light task like folding towels. If the behavior continues, try to remain patient and remember that this is a result of changing patterns in the brain, not a deliberate attempt to be difficult. Staying calm and providing consistent reassurance can help the elder feel more secure, which may naturally reduce the frequency of the repetitive questions over time.
