## Struggling to Find the Right Words

It is common for anyone to occasionally have a word "on the tip of their tongue," but for some elders, this becomes a persistent struggle. You might observe them forgetting simple, everyday words or substituting them with inappropriate or unusual terms. This can make their speech difficult for others to follow and may lead to the elder withdrawing from conversations because they find it hard to keep up or join in.

**Daily Life Example:** During a family dinner, the elder might refer to a "watch" as a "hand-clock" or simply stop speaking mid-sentence, unable to find the next word. They might repeat the same phrase multiple times or lose the thread of the discussion entirely.

**Gentle Supportive Action:** When the elder is struggling, give them extra time and a quiet environment without distractions. Avoid the urge to finish their sentences for them immediately. Instead, listen patiently and offer comfort. If they are stuck, you might gently say, "Take your time, I am listening," or suggest a related word to help them continue their thought.
