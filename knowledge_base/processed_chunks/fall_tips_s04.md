## Kitchen Safety and Organization

In the kitchen, the goal is to minimize the need for reaching, stretching, or using stools. You might consider reorganizing cabinets so that frequently used items—such as cups, plates, and snacks—are stored at waist or chest height. This reduces the risk of the elder losing balance while reaching for high shelves or bending too low. Spills should be cleaned up immediately to prevent the floor from becoming a hazard. If the floor surface is naturally slippery, applying a non-slip coating or using low-pile, secured mats in work areas can be helpful. If you observe the elder using a chair or a step-stool to reach items, it is a prompt to move those items to a more accessible location. Encouraging the elder to use a kitchen trolley can also help them move items safely without having to carry them while walking.
