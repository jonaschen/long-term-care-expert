## Placing Belongings in Unusual Spots

It is common to misplace an item occasionally, but the elder might begin putting objects in very strange or illogical locations. Furthermore, they may lose the ability to "retrace their steps" to find what they have lost. In some cases, because they cannot find the item and do not remember where they put it, they may become suspicious and accuse family members or caregivers of stealing.

**Daily Life Example:** You might find the elder's eyeglasses inside the sugar jar, or discover a flowerpot has been moved into the refrigerator. They might hide their wallet for safety and then, minutes later, forget the hiding spot and feel distressed that someone has taken it.

**Gentle Supportive Action:** Create designated "home" spots for essential items like keys, remotes, and wallets. Use high-visibility labels or bright baskets to make these spots easy to see. If an item is lost, try to help the elder look for it calmly without making them feel at fault. Having duplicates of frequently lost items, like reading glasses, can also reduce anxiety for both the elder and the family.
