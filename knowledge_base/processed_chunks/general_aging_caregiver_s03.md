## Talking With Other Family Members About Caregiving Responsibilities

Caregiving should not fall on the shoulders of just one person. Effective support for an elder often requires a 'team' mindset, yet many families struggle to talk openly about how to divide these responsibilities. Initiating a family meeting can provide a structured space to discuss the elder's changing patterns and the practical needs of the household. It is helpful to approach these conversations with a focus on shared goals rather than blame, acknowledging that every family member has different strengths and limitations.

When discussing tasks, consider dividing them into specific roles: someone might handle financial planning and administrative tasks, another might manage grocery shopping and meal preparation, while another provides the primary physical presence in the home. Even family members who live far away can contribute by making regular check-in calls or coordinating appointments. Using a shared calendar or a group messaging app can help keep everyone informed about the elder's daily rhythm and any upcoming needs. By clearly defining who is responsible for what, you reduce the risk of one person becoming overwhelmed and ensure that the elder is surrounded by a supportive network of family. Remember, asking for help from your relatives is a way of honoring the collective responsibility of the family to care for its elders.
