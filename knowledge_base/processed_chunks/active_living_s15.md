## Daily Flexibility and Cool-Down Stretches

Flexibility is important for keeping joints moving smoothly and preventing the feeling of stiffness. Stretches are best performed when the muscles are warm, making them ideal as a cool-down at the end of an activity session. Each stretch should be held for 10 to 30 seconds without bouncing.

Helpful stretches include reaching for the toes while seated, stretching the arms across the chest, and gentle side bends. The elder should feel a slight pull in the muscle but no pain. If the elder seems to be straining, remind them to relax and only stretch as far as is comfortable. Regular stretching can improve the elder's range of motion, making it easier to do things like put on shoes or reach for items behind them. Integrating these short stretching sessions into the daily routine—perhaps before bed or after a walk—supports long-term physical comfort.
