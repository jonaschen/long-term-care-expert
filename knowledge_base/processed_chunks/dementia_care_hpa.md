## Core Principles: Security and Regularity
- **Environment:** Keep surroundings familiar. Use clear text/image labels on doors (e.g., Toilet, Kitchen). Ensure adequate lighting and remove clutter.
- **Routine:** Establish a fixed daily rhythm (meals, toilet, sleep) to reduce anxiety and time-sense confusion.
- **Nutrition:** Observe if the elder forgets to eat or drink. Use transparent cups and small, frequent meals.

## Communication Skills
- **Simplicity:** Use short, specific instructions. One task at a time.
- **Non-verbal:** Use gentle eye contact, gestures, and allow at least 20 seconds for a response.
- **Encouragement:** Follow the "Three Times Principle": go slow, one thing at a time, and give timely praise.

## Behavioral Observation (4R Principle)
When observing repetitive behavior, delusions, or agitation:
1. **Reassure:** Use a gentle tone to make the elder feel safe.
2. **Reconsider:** Think about the cause (physical discomfort, noisy environment).
3. **Redirect:** Shift focus to an interesting or familiar activity.
4. **Relax:** The caregiver must remain calm for the elder to settle.

## Activity Suggestions
- **Reminiscence:** Use familiar activities like old songs, photo albums, or simple chores to build a sense of participation.
