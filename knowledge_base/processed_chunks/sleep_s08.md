## Safe Nighttime Movement and Fall Prevention

Because elders often need to wake up during the night, ensuring their safety during movement is a top priority for sleep-related care. Waking up in a dark, quiet room can occasionally lead to disorientation, increasing the risk of stumbles or falls. To support safe movement, the environment should be adapted to the elder's needs. This includes installing motion-sensor lights or dim nightlights that lead from the bedside to the bathroom.

Pathways should be kept completely clear of clutter, electrical cords, or loose rugs. It is also helpful for the elder to sit on the edge of the bed for a minute before standing up; this allows their blood pressure to stabilize and reduces feelings of lightheadedness. Keeping necessary items, such as a glass of water, a flashlight, or a mobile phone, within easy reach of the bed can also minimize the need for unnecessary movement. Families can help by checking the height of the bed—it should be easy for the elder to get in and out of without straining. By prioritizing a safe environment, both the elder and the family can feel more at ease during the night. This sense of security itself can contribute to a more relaxed state, making it easier for the elder to return to sleep after a brief wake-up.
